conclusionHeading: The Systems Thinking
conclusion: |
  Stepping up as co-DRI alongside the program lead meant absorbing coordination responsibilities while continuing production work in parallel. A QA system went up that made the complexity legible: every page tracked, every market accounted for, every stakeholder knowing exactly what they needed and by when. When the launch date moved up by nearly a week, the review schedule was recalibrated and revised deadlines communicated to PMMs, international partners, Creative, and Production, without drama, without delay.

  The most consequential moment came in realizing that localized packaging imagery simply didn't exist yet for certain markets. Sourcing it proactively, before anyone had identified the gap, prevented post-launch corrections that would have cost time and trust. That's the kind of work that's invisible when it goes right and very visible when it doesn't.

  The most telling signal came after launch: the hardware product marketing managers reached out directly to say it was the most relaxed and professional launch experience they'd ever had with our team. A compressed global launch doesn't succeed through heroics. It succeeds through ruthless clarity, and the willingness to build that clarity when no one else has.
